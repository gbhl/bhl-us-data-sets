Monday, February 1, 1869.
Clear but rather cold.
Ther. about 20[degrees] - 32[degrees] - 
20[degrees]. Rose as usual.
Walked down with
Farrar in A.M.
Did not write any
paper to day.
Got dismissed at
recess and studied
as soon as we got
home from school.
Then put on my
skates and skated
all the P.M. in our 
yard. Practised
particularly on
the cross cut with turn
backwards. In the
evening R. Deane
called and stayed
till 10 P.M.
Tuesday, February 2, 1869.
  Clear but cold.
Ther. about 10[degrees] - 28[degrees] - 20[degrees].
Rose as usual was
attacked by a sudden
dizziness at school and
had to be dismissed at
the end of the third hour.
Spent all of the afternoon
in the house lying down
most of the time. H.
Henshaw called and
spent an hour or so.
took a bath and some
medicine before going
to bed.
