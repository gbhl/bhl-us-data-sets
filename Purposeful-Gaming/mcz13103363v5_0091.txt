Monday, June 21, 1869.
  A.M. clear and warm.
P.M. cloudy & rather
cold. Ther. about 72[degrees] - 
65[degrees] - 60[degrees]. Rose as usual.
After breakfast studied
a little when H. Henshaw
came up and spent
most of the forenoon
with me. After he
left spent several hours
studying on my
Geometry. Wrote up
my diary in P.M.
Had green peas to day
from the farm.
Tuesday, June 22, 1869.
  Cloudy & rained most
of the day. Ther. about
50[degrees] - 60[degrees] - 60[degrees].
Rose at about 8 A.M.
Spent the whole day
in the house & studied
a good deal of the time.
In the evening went in
to see Mr. Davenport &
he made me up a fine
collection of woods.
