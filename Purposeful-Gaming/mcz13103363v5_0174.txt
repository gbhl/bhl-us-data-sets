Saturday, December 4, 1869.
  Cloudy and snowed
nearly all day but very
little snow fell.
Ther. about 2[degrees] - 18[degrees] - 
20[degrees]. Rose as usual.
After breakfast went
up with Mr. Danforth
on his wagon and
spent the whole day
overseeing the moving.
Came down once
in the carryall and
brought a draw of
eggs in addition to
a number of birds.
Today finishd up
the moving with the
exception of one
load which is to be tak-
en Wednesday.
R. Deane called in
the evening and spent
several hours.
Sunday, December 5, 1869.
  Cloudy and sprin-
kled a good deal.
Ther. about 30[degrees] - 32[degrees] - 
30[degrees]. Rose as usual.
Attended church
in A.M. walking
down with John and
Charley. Walked up
with J. Russell.
In P.M. walked up
to the pond wth C.
Carter and met R.
Deane and E. Dodge
there. We walked
home with them and
spent an hour or
two at R. Deane's.
Talked most of
the evening.
