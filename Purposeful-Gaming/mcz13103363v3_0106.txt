Friday, July 19, 1867.
  Cloudy nearly all day but
the sun shone out occasionally.
Ther. about 60 degrees, 70 degrees, 65 degrees.
Had breakfast at 7.30 A.M.
After breakfast stuffed the
female long-eared owl
commencing at 8.30 &
finishing at 11.30 A.M.
Then went up to see
C. Carter and stayed till
dinner time. Had dinner
at 3.15 P.M. & then rode
down on the car to Mr. Brad[bury]'s
house. He kept me for
about an hour. When
I got home put the
eye[s] into my owl. Then
went up to the Nichols'
again & stayed till tea time.

Saturday, July 20, 1867.
  Cloudy all day and rained
steadily nearly all the
time. Ther. about
58 degrees, 60 degrees, 58 degrees.
Rose at the usual time.
Spent nearly all the
A.M. in stuffing the young
long-eared owl shot
Thursday. Finished the
owl at about 1. Then had
dinner. We intended to
take the 3 P.M. train for
Rye Beach but did not on
account of the rain. Read
till 5.30 P.M. Then went
up to see R. Deane. He walked
down with me and stayed
till 8 P.M. Studied Greek
most of the evening.
