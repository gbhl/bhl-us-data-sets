Thursday, June 3, 1869.
  Clear and very warm.
Ther. about 70[degrees] - 80[degrees] - 
70[degrees]. Rode down to
school but got late
as usual. We write
either a Latin or Greek
grammer paper every
day now. Spent all
the P.M. on my
Homer, reading 250
lines. In the evening
rode down to the
pond with Mr. Dav.
and took a swim
meeting the Russells'
& several others who came
across in Theo's boat.
Then rode over to N. Avenue
to see Mr. Myers but he
was not in.
Chordieles Popitue, heard one.
D. Striata, saw a few stragglers.
Purvirus Americana, heard several
Chrysomitris Tristis, saw a
male building.
Friday, June 4, 1869.
  Clear and rather warm.
Ther. about 70[degrees] - 78[degrees] - 
78[degrees]. Rose as usual.
Rode down to school
in A.M. and got
late. Got out at recess.
Stopped in at Washburn's
& four of us read the Homer
lesson for Monday.
After getting home had
lunch, then dressed &
went down to the Hors-
ford's where I spent the
rest of the afternoon playing
croquet with Trudie &
Miss Willis. R. Dean &
J. Croswell were there.
Then went up to the
Dean's & stayed till 10
P.M. & missing the car
had to walk home.
