Thursday, September 9, 1869.
Clear but very windy.
Ther. about 65[degrees] - 70[degrees] - 
65. Rose as usual.
After breakfast H.
Henshaw called and
spent the forenoon
with me. Took him
down as far as Old
Cambridge in the buggy,
Spent the P.M. in
the house.
The country is
everywhere marred by
the violence of yesterday's
storm.
Friday, September 10, 1869.
  Clear and pleasant.
Ther. about 60[degrees] - 68[degrees] - 60[degrees].
Spent the A.M. in the
house and copied
from my diary into
my ledger. In the
P.M. rode up to West
Newton in the buggy
& called to see Maynard
but he was not in.
Still feeling miserably.
Chaetura Pelasgia, saw
several flying Southward.
